### **2. Types of Trading**

There are several types of trading, each with its own time horizon, risk level, and strategy. Hereâ€™s a breakdown:

#### **a. Day Trading**
- **Timeframe:** Positions are opened and closed within the same day.
- **Goal:** Profit from intraday price movements.
- **Tools:** Technical analysis, chart patterns, and real-time data.
- **Risk:** High, due to frequent trading and leverage.

- **Risk:** Moderate.
- **Example:** Buying a stock after a pullback and selling it when it reaches resistance.

- **Goal:** Profit from small price changes.
- **Tools:** High-frequency trading algorithms, Level 2 data.
- **Risk:** Very high, due to high leverage and transaction costs.
- **Example:** Buying and selling a stock multiple times within an hour.

- **Timeframe:** Positions are held for weeks to months.
- **Goal:** Profit from long-term trends.
- **Tools:** Fundamental analysis and macroeconomic trends.
- **Risk:** Low to moderate.
- **Example:** Holding a stock during a bull market.

### **1. What is a Candlestick?**

A candlestick is a type of price chart that displays the **open, high, low, and close (OHLC)** prices of an asset for a specific time period (e.g., 1 minute, 1 hour, 1 day). Each candlestick represents the price action during that period.

---

### **2. Structure of a Candlestick**

A candlestick has two main parts:

#### **a. Body**
- Represents the price range between the **open** and **close**.
- **Bullish Candle (Green/White):** Close > Open (price went up).
- **Bearish Candle (Red/Black):** Close < Open (price went down).

#### **b. Wicks (or Shadows)**
- The thin lines above and below the body.
- **Upper Wick:** Represents the **high** of the period.
- **Lower Wick:** Represents the **low** of the period.

### **4. Types of Candlesticks**

Candlesticks come in various shapes and sizes, each conveying different information about market sentiment.

#### **a. Marubozu**
- **Bullish Marubozu:** No upper or lower wick. The body covers the entire range (open = low, close = high).
  - Indicates strong buying pressure.
- **Bearish Marubozu:** No upper or lower wick. The body covers the entire range (open = high, close = low).
  - Indicates strong selling pressure.

#### **b. Doji**
- The open and close are nearly the same, resulting in a very small body.
- Indicates indecision in the market.
- **Types:**
  - **Standard Doji:** Open and close are exactly the same.
  - **Long-Legged Doji:** Long upper and lower wicks.
  - **Dragonfly Doji:** No upper wick, long lower wick.
  - **Gravestone Doji:** No lower wick, long upper wick.

#### **c. Spinning Top**
- Small body with long upper and lower wicks.
- Indicates indecision or a balance between buyers and sellers.

#### **d. Hammer and Hanging Man**
- **Hammer:** Bullish reversal pattern. Small body, long lower wick, little to no upper wick.
  - Forms after a downtrend.
- **Hanging Man:** Bearish reversal pattern. Same as a hammer but forms after an uptrend.

#### **e. Shooting Star and Inverted Hammer**
- **Shooting Star:** Bearish reversal pattern. Small body, long upper wick, little to no lower wick.
  - Forms after an uptrend.
- **Inverted Hammer:** Bullish reversal pattern. Same as a shooting star but forms after a downtrend.

---

### **5. Common Candlestick Patterns**

Candlestick patterns are formed by one or more candlesticks and can signal potential reversals or continuations in price.

#### **a. Single Candlestick Patterns**
- **Hammer:** Bullish reversal.
- **Hanging Man:** Bearish reversal.
- **Shooting Star:** Bearish reversal.
- **Doji:** Indecision.

#### **b. Two-Candlestick Patterns**
- **Bullish Engulfing:** A small bearish candle followed by a larger bullish candle that "engulfs" the previous candle.
  - Bullish reversal signal.
- **Bearish Engulfing:** A small bullish candle followed by a larger bearish candle that "engulfs" the previous candle.
  - Bearish reversal signal.

#### **c. Three-Candlestick Patterns**
- **Morning Star:** A bearish candle, followed by a small-bodied candle (doji or spinning top), and then a large bullish candle.
  - Bullish reversal signal.
- **Evening Star:** A bullish candle, followed by a small-bodied candle (doji or spinning top), and then a large bearish candle.
  - Bearish reversal signal.

### **6. How to Use Candlesticks in Trading**

#### **c. Confirm with Indicators**
